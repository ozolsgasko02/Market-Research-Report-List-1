Meanwhile, electronic wearable organs leverage technology to perform physiological tasks, often incorporating sensors and software to monitor or regulate bodily functions, like insulin pumps or pacemakers. Both types aim to improve patients' quality of life by enhancing or replacing lost biological functions.</p></p>
