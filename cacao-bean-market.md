<p><p>The cacao bean market is primarily divided into two types: Forastero and Criollo. Forastero beans dominate global production due to their resilience and higher yield, making them more economical and widely available. They are commonly used in mass-produced chocolate. In contrast, Criollo beans are rarer and prized for their complex flavor profile, resulting in premium quality chocolate. Their limited supply and unique taste command higher prices, appealing to gourmet markets and artisanal chocolatiers seeking distinctive products.</p></p>
