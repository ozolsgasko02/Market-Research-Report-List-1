<p><p>Fiber optic cable assemblies come in two primary types: single mode and multi-mode. Single mode cables utilize a single light path, allowing for long-distance transmission with minimal signal loss, making them ideal for telecommunications and data centers. In contrast, multi-mode cables have multiple light paths, accommodating shorter distances and higher data rates, suitable for local area networks and shorter connections. Both types are essential in modern communication infrastructure, each optimized for specific applications and performance requirements.</p></p>
